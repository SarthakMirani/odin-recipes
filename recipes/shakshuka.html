Shakshuka

Shakshuka is a North African and Middle Eastern dish with roots tracing back centuries. Its exact origin is debated, but it is widely believed to have originated in the Maghreb region, which includes Tunisia, Algeria, and Libya. The name "shakshuka" comes from a Maghrebi Arabic word meaning "a mixture," reflecting its combination of poached eggs in a spiced tomato and pepper sauce. Some food historians suggest that it was influenced by Ottoman cuisine, which introduced tomato-based stews to the region.

The dish gained widespread popularity when North African Jewish immigrants brought it to Israel, where it became a beloved staple. Over time, shakshuka evolved with regional variations, incorporating ingredients like feta cheese, spinach, or merguez sausage. Today, it is enjoyed worldwide as a hearty breakfast or brunch dish, known for its bold flavors and simple yet satisfying preparation.

Ingredients

  • Olive oil - for sautéing
  • Onion - finely chopped
  • Garlic - minced
  • Bell peppers - usually red, diced
  • Tomatoes - fresh or canned, crushed
  • Eggs - poached directly in the sauce
  • Spices - typically cumin, paprika, and chili powder for warmth
  • Salt and black pepper - to taste
  • Fresh herbs - parsley or cilantro for garnish

Steps

  1. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onion and cook until soft and translucent.
  3. Stir in garlic and cook for another 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add diced red bell pepper and cook for about 5 minutes until softened.
  5. Pour in crushed tomatoes and stir well.
  6. Add cumin, paprika, chili powder, salt, and black pepper.
  7. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens.
  8. Using a spoon, create small wells in the sauce and crack an egg into each well.
  9. Cover the pan and let the eggs cook for 5–7 minutes, or until the whites are set but yolks are slightly runny.
  10. Sprinkle with fresh parsley or cilantro and serve hot with bread or pita.
