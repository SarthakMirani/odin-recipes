Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji originated in Mumbai, India, in the mid-19th century during the textile mill boom. It was created as a quick and filling meal for mill workers who had limited lunch breaks. Street vendors near the mills improvised by mashing a variety of leftover vegetables and mixing them with spices, serving the dish with buttered pav (bread rolls). This made for a nutritious and affordable meal that was easy to eat on the go.

Over time, Pav Bhaji gained popularity beyond the mills and became a beloved street food across India. Vendors began experimenting with different variations, adding ingredients like cheese, paneer, and even fusion elements. Today, it is a staple in Indian cuisine, enjoyed in restaurants and homes alike, often served with a generous dollop of butter and chopped onions.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp butter (for cooking)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper (capsicum), finely chopped
  • 2 potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • ½ cup cauliflower, finely chopped
  • ½ cup green peas
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 ½ tsp pav bhaji masala
  • 1 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • ½ cup water (adjust for consistency)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Boil potatoes, cauliflower, and green peas in a pressure cooker or a pot until soft.
  2. Mash them well using a potato masher and set aside.
  3. Heat butter in a large pan or kadhai on medium heat.
  4. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  5. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes and bell pepper, and cook until soft and mushy.
  7. Mix in pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric, and salt. Stir well.
  8. Add the mashed vegetables and mix everything together.
  9. Pour ½ cup water and mash further to get a smooth consistency. Simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  10. Add lemon juice, mix well, and garnish with chopped coriander.
  11. Serve with butter-toasted pav buns and garnish with butter and chopped onions.
